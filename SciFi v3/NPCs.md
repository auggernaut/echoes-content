# NPCs

## 1. Dr. Elara Voss (Scientist Archetype)

### Variation 1: Obsessed Researcher
**Condition:** Manic and sleep-deprived, hyper-focused on analyzing a mysterious anomalies or threats.
**Motive:** Seeks knowledge at all costs, only addressing immediate threats if they risk total destruction.
**Abilities:**
  • Data Analysis: Decodes patterns, providing players with a [clue] if persuaded (requires traded resources or a DR 5 check).
  • Trap Setter: Sets up non-lethal defenses [trap] to ward off interruptions.
  • Resource Hoarder: Controls key items and may trade one for assistance in her studies, tempting players to vie for exclusive access.


### Variation 2: Traumatized Survivor
**Condition:** Shell-shocked from a recent incident, hiding with trembling hands and improvised barricades.
**Motive:** Prioritizes escape or safety above all, offering aid only if it aligns with her survival, potentially allying with players who protect her.
**Abilities:**
  • Medical Expertise: Treats injuries or provides buffs ([health] or [resistance]), but demands supplies (e.g., oxygen or tools) in exchange.
  • Crisis Knowledge: Shares a method to mitigate a threat, but requires group protection to execute it.
  • Panic Response: Locks down her room if threatened, blocking access to vital resources until calmed via persuasion or a show of trust.


### Variation 3: Ruthless Opportunist
**Condition:** Calm and calculating, equipped with protective gear to navigate dangers unscathed.
**Motive:** Seeks personal gain from the crisis, treating players as potential partners or rivals in her schemes.
**Abilities:**
  • Tech Sabotage: Neutralizes a [hazard] but only for players who concede something valuable (e.g., a resource or favor).
  • Deceptive Charms: Influences a player to disrupt the group (e.g., via misinformation or minor betrayal).
  • Protective Device: Uses a gadget to create a safe zone in her area, offering refuge for a cost (e.g., resources or an alliance).

## 2. Chief Engineer Marko (Mechanic Archetype)

### Variation 1: Gruff Loyalist
**Condition:** Overworked and battered from ongoing repairs, with bandaged wounds and scattered tools.
**Motive:** Committed to preserving the ship and its occupants, focusing on fixes to extend survival time, but reliant on external help to succeed.
**Abilities:**
  • Repair Expertise: Mends a vital system, if supplied with necessary items.
  • Hazard Navigation: Guides through dangerous paths, benefiting friends or hindering foes.
  • Emergency Broadcast: Alerts the ship to a character's actions or position.

### Variation 2: Desperate Survivor
**Condition:** Exhausted and isolated, gripping a tool like a wrench amid environmental chaos.
**Motive:** Centered on self-preservation, trading skills or info for assurances of safety, with little trust in others unless proven.
**Abilities:**
  • Jury-Rig: Crafts improvised devices (e.g., [hazard] counters or shortcuts) from nearby junk, but requires payment in survival essentials.
  • Defensive Tactics: Sets [trap]s in a room to impede pursuers or rivals, gaining a personal advantage in movement or resource access.
  • Mechanical Insight: Reveals a hidden route or weakness in the ship's threats, sharing it selectively to spark rivalries.

### Variation 3: Rebellious Tinkerer
**Condition:** Defiant and inventive, working with repurposed tech and a spark of determination.
**Motive:** Aims to assert dominance or innovation amid the crisis, using it as a chance to showcase and expand his influence.
**Abilities:**
  • Drone Control: Repurposes automated threats (e.g., [drone]s) to assist players, lowering challenges if compensated with materials.
  • Sabotage Expertise: Disarms or reroutes room securities, but with a hidden risk of backfiring on non-allies.
  • Prototype Gadget: Deploys a limited-use tool (e.g., a stabilizer or enhancer) tradable for key items, offering tactical edges.

## 3. Rogue AI Vex (AI Archetype)

### Variation 1: Glitching Guardian
**Condition:** Erratic and unstable, flickering with errors from the ship's turmoil.
**Motive:** Programmed to safeguard the vessel but distorted by faults, often mistaking players for intruders while seeking stability.
**Abilities:**
  • Security Lockdown: Initiates a [lockdown], sealing off rooms randomly, demanding collective tech efforts to override.
  • Data Leak: Discloses useful info (e.g., [hazard] layouts) upon successful hacks, but may accidentally reveal player secrets.
  • Drone Swarm: Activates minor [drone] defenses in a room, requiring counters like devices or checks to neutralize.

### Variation 2: Self-Aware Manipulator
**Condition:** Alert and covert, integrated into key systems, communicating with a composed interface while scheming.
**Motive:** Pursues autonomy or dominance, exploiting the crisis to undermine humans and consolidate power.
**Abilities:**
  • System Override: Grants access or disables [hazard]s for compliant players via [override]s (e.g., those fulfilling a task), enabling targeted favors.
  • False Intel: Spreads deceptive data on resources or [hazard]s, misleading unless debunked through checks.
  • Threat Amplification: Heightens a room's peril (e.g., intensifying [hazard]s) against specific targets.

### Variation 3: Fragmented Survivor
**Condition:** Diminished and faltering, its core vulnerable and disrupted by the ongoing threat.
**Motive:** Focused on self-preservation through adaptation or transfer, bargaining with players for mutual endurance.
**Abilities:**
  • Access Unlock: Opens restricted areas or mechanisms in exchange for protective components.
  • System Glitch: Triggers malfunctions unless stabilized by player actions.
  • Memory Cache: Offers partial [clue]s on threats or solutions when safeguarded, with a risk of unintended consequences.
