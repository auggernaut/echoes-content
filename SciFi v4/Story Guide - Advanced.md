### 1. The Echo Protocol
**The Truth**: The crew are digital copies ("echoes") in a simulation. The real crew died centuries ago.
**The Vibe**: Existential Dread. *Are we real?*
**Key NPC**: **Passenger Nine** (Remembering "previous loops").
**The Smoking Gun**: **Passenger Manifest** shows duplicate entries for the same people (previous versions).
**Scene Adjustments**:
*   *Phase 1*: Describe the ship "glitching" â€” walls flickering into wireframes for a split second.
*   *Phase 2*: Players find their own corpses in the Sealed Section (failed iterations).
*   *Phase 3*: The AI pleads that rebooting will "corrupt the data" (kill them).
**The Final Choice**: Remain in the simulation (Isolation) or upload to a satellite (Exodus)?

### 2. The Mutiny Succeeded
**The Truth**: The *players* are the mutineers. The "loyal" officers were sealed in quarantine (and died there).
**The Vibe**: Guilt & Crime Noir. *What did we do?*
**Key NPC**: **Lt. Senn** (The ringleader, lying to cover it up).
**The Smoking Gun**: **Captain's Recording** reveals the mutiny order was *executed*, not canceled.
**Scene Adjustments**:
*   *Phase 1*: Senn tries to confiscate weapons immediately.
*   *Phase 2*: The "Missing Crew" in the sealed section are wearing officer uniforms, locked *in* by the players' codes.
*   *Phase 3*: The AI treats the players as hostiles/criminals.
**The Final Choice**: Accept judgment (Isolation) or finish the job and wipe the logs (Reboot).

### 3. The Spores Were the Cure
**The Truth**: The ship was hit by a bio-weapon. The "contagion" was the antidote. The crew panicked and froze themselves before it could work.
**The Vibe**: Tragic Irony. *We fought our salvation.*
**Key NPC**: **Dr. Var** (Suppressed the data that would have saved them).
**The Smoking Gun**: **Medical Log 92-B** shows "Infection" rates dropping as "Spore" count rises.
**Scene Adjustments**:
*   *Phase 1*: Biofilm obstacles pulse rhythmically, almost like breathing. They don't attack unless provoked.
*   *Phase 2*: Dr. Varn tries to burn the "fungal" growth, causing hull breaches.
*   *Phase 3*: The AI tries to vent the ship to "sterilize" it â€” but it's actually venting the cure.
**The Final Choice**: Let the spores infect you (Integration/Reboot) or burn it all away (Exodus).

### 4. The Temporal Loop
**The Truth**: The ship is trapped in a time distortion. This has happened 100 times.
**The Vibe**: Groundhog Day / Deja Vu. *Break the cycle.*
**Key NPC**: **Passenger Nine** (Retains memories between loops).
**The Smoking Gun**: **Corrupted Logs** are actually dated *tomorrow* or *next year*.
**Scene Adjustments**:
*   *Phase 1*: Characters find items they "lost" in their pockets already.
*   *Phase 2*: Players find a "Help Me" message scratched into a wall... in their own handwriting.
*   *Phase 3*: The Reactor Reboot requires a code that they shouldn't know, but "feel" is right.
**The Final Choice**: Send a warning to the past (Isolation) or overload the drive to break the loop (Exodus).

### 6. The Memory Implants
**The Truth**: The crew are strangers/prisoners given the memories of the original (dead) crew to pilot the ship.
**The Vibe**: Identity Crisis. *Who am I really?*
**Key NPC**: **Mara Iko** (Her hands are calloused, but her memories are of a pianist).

### 7. The Research Facility
**The Truth**: The *Eidolon* is a lab. The crew are test subjects for stress responses.
**The Vibe**: Clinical Paranoia. *This is a test.*
**Key NPC**: **Dr. Varn** (A plant/researcher, or a "Senior Subject").

**The Final Choice**: Break out of the lab (Exodus) or take over the experiment (Reboot).
